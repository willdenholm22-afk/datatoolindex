# Monday.com Review (2026): Pricing, Features, and Verdict

Monday.com is worth it for **non-technical operations, sales, and marketing teams** that need visual project tracking without a developer to configure it. At a starting price of $99/month (for the Basic tier's minimum seat count as of April 2026), it's priced above lightweight tools like Trello but below enterprise PPM suites. It's the wrong choice for engineering teams wanting code-native issue tracking (use Linear or Jira) and for sub-$100/month budgets (use ClickUp Free or Trello). Pricing scales per-seat and tiered-by-feature, which can surprise buyers who hit automation or integration limits mid-quarter.

## What Monday.com Is

Monday.com is a cloud-based visual work management platform built around customizable "boards" — spreadsheet-like grids where rows are items (tasks, deals, tickets) and columns are typed fields (status, owner, date, number, formula). As verified April 2026, it serves adjacent use cases beyond project management: CRM, marketing campaign tracking, HR workflows, and sales-ops approval cycles. The core appeal is the low onboarding curve for users coming from Excel — the grid feels familiar, while status columns, automations, and dashboards add structure email threads can't. Monday is cloud-only (no self-hosted option as of Q2 2026) and is delivered via three product SKUs: Work Management, Sales CRM, and Dev. This review focuses on Work Management, the flagship.

## Pricing (verified 2026-04-18)

Monday.com uses **per-seat + tiered feature gating**, with a **3-seat minimum** on paid plans. Prices below are annual billing, per seat, per month.

| Tier | Price/seat/mo (annual) | Min seats | Key limits |
|---|---|---|---|
| Free | $0 | 2 users max | 3 boards, no automations |
| Basic | $9 | 3 | Unlimited items, no timeline/Gantt, no automations |
| Standard | $12 | 3 | Timeline, calendar, 250 automation actions/mo |
| Pro | $19 | 3 | Private boards, time tracking, 25k automations/mo |
| Enterprise | Contact vendor | Contact vendor | SSO, audit log, HIPAA, advanced governance |

Source: [monday.com/pricing](https://monday.com/pricing), verified 2026-04-18.

**Notes:**
- The $99/mo entry point reflects Standard tier × 3 seats × annual (~$108 actual, rounded). Monthly billing runs ~18% higher.
- Automation and integration actions are **account-wide pools**, not per-seat — heavy automation users hit caps on Standard.
- Guest users are free on Pro+ but counted against seat count on Basic/Standard under certain configurations (verify with sales).
- No usage-based pricing; no free-forever tier for >2 users.

## Features

**Boards and views**
- Grid (default), Kanban, Timeline/Gantt, Calendar, Chart, Workload, Map views
- 30+ typed column types (status, people, date, formula, mirror, dependency, etc.)
- Subitems (one level of nesting)

**Automation and integrations**
- No-code recipe-based automations (if/then triggers)
- Native integrations with Slack, Gmail, Outlook, Teams, Jira, GitHub, Salesforce, HubSpot (full list: [monday.com/integrations](https://monday.com/integrations), verified 2026-04-18)
- Webhooks and REST API available on all paid tiers

**Reporting**
- Dashboards aggregating multiple boards (widget count gated by tier)
- Workload view for capacity planning (Pro+)

**Governance (Enterprise)**
- SAML SSO, SCIM, audit log, IP restrictions, HIPAA compliance
- Panel-level permissions and content directory

**Not included (verify if critical):**
- Native budgeting/financials (requires integration)
- Advanced resource leveling (Pro workload view is basic)
- Self-hosted or on-prem deployment

## Best For

- **Sales ops teams managing quote approval cycles** — status columns + automations replace email threads; non-technical reps adopt it without training.
- **Marketing teams tracking campaigns across channels** — timeline views and cross-board dashboards consolidate what otherwise lives in 4 spreadsheets.
- **Operations leaders coming from Excel** — the grid metaphor transfers directly; learning curve measured in hours, not weeks.
- **Cross-functional project coordination (3-50 person teams)** — good middle ground between Trello's simplicity and Jira's complexity.
- **Client-facing agencies** — guest access (Pro+) lets external stakeholders review work without a paid seat.

## Not Ideal For

- **Engineering teams doing issue tracking and sprints** → use [Linear](/tools/linear) or Jira. Monday lacks git integration depth, proper sprint velocity tracking, and a developer-native API surface.
- **Sub-$100/month budgets** → use [ClickUp](/tools/clickup) Free or [Trello](/tools/trello). Monday's 3-seat minimum + tier gating makes it expensive for tiny teams.
- **Complex resource management / PPM** → use Smartsheet or Planview. Monday's workload view is directional, not financially rigorous.
- **Regulated environments needing self-hosted** → Monday is cloud-only. Consider Jira Data Center or self-hosted alternatives.
- **Teams needing deep document collaboration** → use Notion or Coda. Monday Docs exists but is secondary to boards.

## FAQ

**Is Monday.com free?**
A free tier exists for up to 2 users with 3 boards and no automations. Paid plans start at 3 seats minimum ($9/seat/mo Basic, annual billing, verified 2026-04-18).

**Does Monday.com replace a CRM?**
Monday Sales CRM is a separate SKU. It works for small sales teams but lacks the reporting depth of Salesforce or HubSpot. Evaluate directly if CRM is the primary use case.

**Can I self-host Monday.com?**
No. Monday.com is cloud-only as of April 2026. No on-prem or private-cloud option is publicly offered.

**How do automation limits work?**
Automations run from an account-wide monthly pool (250 on Standard, 25,000 on Pro). Each trigger-action run counts as one. Heavy users should model expected volume before committing.

**Is Monday.com HIPAA compliant?**
HIPAA compliance is available only on the Enterprise tier. Contact vendor for a BAA.

## Verdict

Monday.com earns its price for non-technical teams that need visual project tracking and will actually use automations and dashboards. The grid-first UX is its moat — Excel refugees onboard faster than on any competitor. It loses to Linear and Jira for software engineering, to ClickUp on price-per-feature, and to Smartsheet on rigorous PMO work. The real risk is tier creep: buyers start on Basic, hit the automation ceiling within a quarter, and upgrade to Pro — roughly doubling cost. Model automation usage before signing. For sales ops and marketing teams in the 10-200 seat range, it's a defensible default.
