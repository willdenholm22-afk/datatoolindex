# Zilliant Review (2026): Pricing, Features, and Verdict

Zilliant is worth evaluating if you are a mid-to-large industrial B2B, manufacturing, or distribution company with quote-based selling, 10k+ SKUs, and an existing history of quote and order data. Its core value is productizing list-to-deal analysis — the quote-vs-approved-vs-transacted spread work that pricing analysts typically grind through in Excel. It is not worth the spend for fixed-price businesses, services firms, or teams without clean historical transaction data. Expect a 4–6 month implementation and a mid-six-figure annual contract. If you sell fewer than ~1,000 SKUs or lack quote history, look elsewhere (verified April 2026).

## What Zilliant Is

Zilliant is a cloud-based pricing analytics and price optimization platform targeting the industrial B2B and distribution segment. Its flagship capabilities — sold under the IQ product family — focus on price guidance, deal scoring, and list-to-deal tracking across high-SKU-count catalogs. The platform ingests ERP, CRM, and CPQ data (typically SAP, Oracle, Epicor, Infor, Salesforce) and outputs customer-specific target and floor prices, which sales reps consume either in their quoting system or via a Zilliant UI. Unlike horizontal pricing tools, Zilliant is purpose-built for environments where the same customer buys the same SKU repeatedly under negotiated contracts, making statistical price sensitivity modeling viable at the customer-SKU pair level (verified April 2026).

## Best For

- **Industrial distributors with 10k–100k+ SKUs.** Excel pivot tables break at this scale; Zilliant handles customer-SKU pair modeling natively.
- **Manufacturers with negotiated, quote-based B2B selling.** The deal-scoring workflow is designed around repeated quote-to-order cycles, not transactional e-commerce.
- **Pricing teams currently doing manual list-to-deal analysis.** If an analyst spends >20% of their time joining quote data to invoice data in Excel, Zilliant replaces that workflow.
- **Companies with clean 2+ years of historical transaction data.** The models need history. Without it, you're paying for software that can't yet produce guidance.
- **Organizations with executive sponsorship for price realization initiatives.** This is a change management project, not a SaaS install.

## Not Ideal For

- **Fixed-price or catalog-price businesses.** If you don't negotiate, there's no list-to-deal spread to optimize. Use a simpler tool like [Pricefx](/tools/pricefx) or internal BI.
- **Services firms, SaaS, or subscription businesses.** Zilliant is built around tangible-goods transaction data. Look at [Paddle Price Intelligently](/tools/paddle-price-intelligently) or custom analytics instead.
- **Small businesses (<$50M revenue).** The entry price and 4–6 month implementation will not pencil out. Consider [Vendavo](/tools/vendavo) Lite offerings or Excel + a pricing consultant.
- **Companies without historical quote data.** The platform's models are worthless without 18–24 months of clean history. Fix the data capture problem first.
- **Teams needing go-live in <90 days.** No enterprise pricing platform meets that timeline. If it's urgent, start with a BI dashboard.

## FAQ

**How long does Zilliant implementation take?**
Typically 4–6 months for a single business unit. Multi-ERP, multi-BU deployments routinely run 9–12 months. Anyone quoting under 4 months is optimistic (verified April 2026).

**Does Zilliant publish pricing?**
No. Contracts are custom-quoted. Directional ACV ranges from ~$60k to $150k+/year based on practitioner reports, but you must contact the vendor for an actual quote.

**What ERPs does Zilliant integrate with?**
Zilliant documents native connectors for SAP ECC/S4HANA, Oracle, Infor, and Epicor, plus Salesforce CPQ. Other systems typically integrate via REST API or flat-file feeds. Verify current connector list with the vendor.

**Can Zilliant work without historical data?**
Not effectively. The optimization models require 18–24 months of quote and transaction history to produce reliable guidance. Net-new businesses or companies with poor data capture should fix data hygiene first.

**Zilliant vs. Vendavo — which should I pick?**
For industrial distribution with high SKU counts and quote-based selling, Zilliant has the tighter niche fit. For process manufacturing, chemicals, or companies already on SAP where Vendavo has deeper integration, Vendavo often wins. Run both through your actual data in a proof of concept.

## Verdict

Zilliant is a defensible buy for industrial B2B and distribution companies with real list-to-deal spread problems, high SKU counts, and clean transaction history. The niche fit is genuine — this is not a horizontal pricing tool repackaged for manufacturing. The costs are real too: $60k–$150k+/year, a 4–6 month implementation, and meaningful change management with the sales org. For the right buyer, ROI typically comes from 1–3% margin recovery on negotiated deals, which pays the contract back quickly at $100M+ revenue. For the wrong buyer — services firms, fixed-price catalogs, sub-$50M companies — it will be an expensive shelfware story. Evaluate against Vendavo and Pricefx before signing (verified April 2026).
