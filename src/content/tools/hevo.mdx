# Hevo Data Review (2026): Pricing, Features, and Verdict

Hevo is worth evaluating if you're a mid-market team that wants Fivetran-style managed ETL without Fivetran-level pricing, and nobody on staff wants to maintain open-source connectors. At $299/month starting (verified 2026-04-18), it undercuts Fivetran at low-to-mid event volumes and ships 150+ pre-built connectors with a no-code interface. It's a poor fit if you're pushing >1B events/month, need dbt-grade transformation logic, or already have a data engineering team comfortable with Airbyte OSS. Think of Hevo as the stepping-stone ETL for analyst-led teams that haven't yet hired a dedicated data engineer.

## What Hevo Data Is

Hevo Data is a cloud-hosted ETL/ELT platform that moves data from SaaS apps, databases, and file stores into warehouses like Snowflake, BigQuery, Redshift, and Databricks. As of April 2026, Hevo advertises 150+ pre-built connectors and a no-code pipeline builder targeted at analysts rather than data engineers. Pricing is event-based (per row/event ingested per month) rather than per-connector or per-seat. The product includes basic in-flight transformations (Python or drag-and-drop) and schema drift handling, but does not replace a dbt layer for modeling. Hevo is SOC 2, HIPAA, and GDPR compliant per vendor documentation ([hevodata.com/security](https://hevodata.com/security), verified 2026-04-18). Deployment is cloud-only; no self-hosted option exists.

## Features

**Ingestion**
- 150+ pre-built source connectors (SaaS, databases, file stores, streaming) per vendor site, verified 2026-04-18
- Real-time CDC for major databases (MySQL, Postgres, MongoDB, SQL Server)
- Automatic schema drift detection and mapping

**Transformation**
- Drag-and-drop transformation UI
- Python-based custom transformations
- Does **not** replace dbt for warehouse-native modeling

**Destinations**
- Snowflake, BigQuery, Redshift, Databricks, Firebolt, Postgres, S3
- Reverse ETL ("Hevo Activate") sold as a separate product line

**Ops & Governance**
- Pipeline monitoring dashboard with alerts
- Role-based access control
- SOC 2 Type II, HIPAA, GDPR (per vendor docs, 2026-04-18)

## Best For

- **Mid-market analytics teams without a dedicated data engineer.** No-code setup means a senior analyst can own the pipeline stack.
- **Companies replacing Stitch or legacy Informatica.** Hevo's connector catalog covers most common SaaS sources and is notably cheaper than enterprise ETL at <50M events/mo.
- **Teams under 500M events/month.** Below this threshold, Hevo's event-based pricing is typically competitive with Fivetran's MAR model.
- **Analysts comfortable with Power Query / visual ETL.** The UI paradigm translates directly — minimal ramp-up time.
- **Regulated mid-market (healthcare, fintech) needing HIPAA but not full enterprise compliance tooling.** Business Critical tier covers this.

## Not Ideal For

- **Very high volume pipelines (>1B events/month).** Event-based pricing gets punishing at scale — evaluate [Fivetran](/tools/fivetran) on MAR or self-hosted [Airbyte OSS](/tools/airbyte).
- **Teams with strong data engineering already in place.** If you're comfortable writing YAML and running containers, Airbyte OSS will run ~60% cheaper at scale.
- **Complex transformation-heavy workloads.** Hevo is not a dbt replacement. Pair with dbt Core/Cloud, or evaluate a Matillion-style transform-first platform.
- **Air-gapped or self-hosted requirements.** Hevo is cloud-only as of April 2026. Use Airbyte OSS or Meltano.
- **Teams needing niche/long-tail connectors.** If your source isn't in the 150+ catalog, Airbyte's 350+ community connectors give better odds ([airbyte.com/connectors](https://airbyte.com/connectors), verified 2026-04-18).

## Alternatives

| Tool | One-line comparison |

| [Fivetran](/tools/fivetran) | More connectors (300+), MAR-based pricing; wins at enterprise scale, loses on price at mid-market |
| [Airbyte Cloud](/tools/airbyte-cloud) | Similar event-based model; larger connector catalog but less polished UX |
| [Airbyte OSS](/tools/airbyte) | Free/self-hosted; ~60% cheaper at scale but requires ~4 hrs/month ops |

## FAQ

**How much does Hevo cost for 50M events/month?**
The Professional plan ($849/mo as of 2026-04-18) covers 20M events; beyond that, contact vendor for custom pricing. Expect roughly $1,500–$2,500/mo at 50M depending on connector mix, but this is not publicly disclosed — get a written quote.

**Does Hevo replace dbt?**
No. Hevo offers in-flight transformations but is not a warehouse-native modeling layer. Most mature stacks run Hevo for EL and dbt for T.

**Is Hevo HIPAA compliant?**
Yes, on the Business Critical tier per vendor documentation ([hevodata.com/security](https://hevodata.com/security), verified 2026-04-18). Starter and Professional tiers are not HIPAA-covered.

**Can I self-host Hevo?**
No. Hevo is cloud-only as of April 2026. For self-hosted, use Airbyte OSS or Meltano.

**How does Hevo compare to Fivetran on price?**
At <20M events/month, Hevo is typically 30–50% cheaper. Above ~500M events/month, Fivetran's MAR-based model often wins because MAR counts unique rows rather than event volume. Model both before committing.

## Verdict

Hevo Data is the right call for mid-market teams that need reliable managed ETL without hiring a data engineer. The $299/mo entry point and no-code UI make it accessible to analyst-led teams, and the 150+ connector catalog covers the common SaaS stack. It's not the cheapest option (Airbyte OSS wins there) or the most scalable (Fivetran wins at enterprise MAR volumes), but it's the most defensible middle-ground choice as of April 2026. Budget 2-4 hours for initial pipeline setup per source. Revisit the vendor decision once you cross 500M events/month or hire your first data engineer.
